# drama916 — demo video script (~2:30)

Goal: a judge watches this and immediately gets *input → pipeline → finished
film*, plus the one differentiator (critic-before-render + cost receipt).
Record 1080p screen capture of the live deploy; talk over it. Keep it moving —
use a film that's already generated for the payoff so you don't wait 10 min on
camera.

Recommended setup: have one fresh logline typed but NOT submitted, and one
already-finished film (e.g. "Gods of the Pitch") ready in Recent generations.

---

### 0:00–0:25 — The problem (cold open: phone b-roll + text cards, no VO)
Shot on a phone, text cards carry the pain — voiceover only enters at the
product. Short raw phrases, lowercase, one thought per card:

- **A · feed (5s)**: a hand swipes a vertical-video feed (use the drama916 rail
  on a phone — our own films, TikTok-like). Muted feed audio.
  Card: **everyone watches**
- **B · tired creator (5s)**: Dan at a desk lit by a monitor, editing timeline
  on screen, rubs his eyes, cold coffee beside.
  Card: **nobody has 4 hours**
- **C · swipe (3s)**: back to the feed, one more swipe.
  Card: **trends don't wait**
- **D · black (2s)**: black screen, one line:
  Card: **what if 1 line was enough?**

### 0:25–0:40 — The promise + the input
> "This is drama916. You type one sentence — and go put the kettle on. While it
> boils, a screenwriter agent writes your script, a critic tears it apart, and a
> director breaks it into shots."

- Show the composer. Click a trending pill → a logline drops in ("these are real
  trends, refreshed hourly — drama916 turns today's internet into story hooks").
- Adjust to a fun one, e.g. *"Ronaldo loses the World Cup and Messi comforts
  him with cheeseburgers."* Pick length + cast. Hit **Action**.

### 0:40–1:15 — The pipeline (the substance)
> "Now it works like a real production — and here's the key idea: it locks the
> story on paper *before* spending a cent on video."

- Show the studio streaming: **Script** writing live → **Board** planning shots →
  **Critic** reviewing. Point at the muted status line.
> "A screenplay agent writes it. A shot planner breaks it into frames — each with
> its own action, dialogue, and camera. Then a critic scores the storyboard for
> continuity and renderability and rewrites the risky shots — while it's still
> text, for a fraction of a cent."

### 1:15–1:45 — The approval gate
> "Before any video renders, you're the director."

- Show the **Storyboard**: the painted frames. Redraw one frame with a note
  ("make him sit alone on the bench") → show the frame + caption update.
> "Redraw any frame, reorder, or drop it. Nothing expensive has happened yet."
- Hit **Film it**.

### 1:45–2:15 — The payoff (cut to the finished film)
> "Each approved frame becomes the first frame of a HappyHorse video clip, every
> line gets a voice, and it's cut into one film."

- Cut to the **premiere** of an already-finished film (don't wait live). Play it
  with sound — the voiced, subtitled 9:16 short.
- Point at the caption + hashtags and the **Copy caption** button.
> "It even writes the TikTok caption and hashtags. One tap to post."

### 2:15–2:30 — The receipt + close
- Point at the cost line under the film.
> "And it shows you the receipt — this whole film cost about five dollars of Qwen
> compute, because the thinking happened in text and the video rendered once.
> Four hours of creator work — done before your kettle boiled.
> One sentence in. A finished drama out. That's drama916."

## Tips
- Do the slow 10-minute render OFF camera; cut straight to a finished film for the
  payoff. Judges want to see the *result*, not a progress bar.
- Keep total under 3:00 (Devpost attention span). 2:30 is ideal.
- Caption the video or narrate clearly — audio may be off on first view.
